About Me

Kurt VonEhr completed his undergrad BSE in Electrical Engineering and a minor in Computer Engineering at GVSU. After working at General Motors for several years, he then pursued his Masters of Engineering in Systems Engineering and Design at the University of Michigan. His engineering interests include: Digital Signal Processing, Communication Theory, Low Power Embedded System Design, Sustainability and Alternative Energy. As Vice President of the GVSU Alternative Energy Club, he has participated in the conversion of a Subaru Outback to an electric vehicle, as well as provided significant contribution of funds through sustainability grants. He is currently leading the dashboard design and control system for an electric motorcycle conversion. Kurt has also performed research focused on piezoelectric ambient energy harvesting and low power micro-controller application design, which has provided valuable experience and insight into realistic future electronics and applications. A "Leave-No-Trace" philosophy is one that he believes in strongly, and he leads by example as often as possible; from bicycling rather than driving, to designing systems with minimum environmental impact in mind. Kurt believes in a sustainable global community, where homes and transportation are powered self-sufficiently or by local sources - made possible through low power electronics and renewable energy.
