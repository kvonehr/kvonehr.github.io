About Me

Kurt VonEhr is a 4th year ECE Student and 1st year IEEE member. He has completed a minor in Computer Engineering and expects to graduate with a B.S in Electrical Engineering, Summer 2015. Engineering interests include: Digital Signal Processing, Communication Theory, Low Power Embedded System Design, Sustainability and Alternative Energy. As member in the Alternative Energy Club, he participated in an electric vehicle conversion, as well as aided in a significant contribution of funds through writing grant applications. Additionally, he led the dashboard design and contributed to the control system for an electric motorcycle conversion. Research focused on piezoelectric energy harvesting and low power microcontroller application design has provided Kurt with valuable experience and insight into realistic future electronics and applications. A "Leave-No-Trace" philosophy is one that Kurt believes in strongly, and he leads by example as often as possible; from bicycling rather than driving, to designing systems with minimum environmental impact in mind. Kurt believes in a sustainable global community, where homes and transportation are powered self-sufficiently or by local sources - made possible through low power electronics and renewable energy.

An Effort to Exceed the Dilettante

To be recruited for employment on credentials alone is often indicative of an organization which undervalues the attitudes and beliefs of its employees. To see the employee as simply a position to be filled, results in a workplace full of turnover and waste. Robust relationships between businesses are whittled away and what remains is a workforce lacking pride in its work. If you, the employer, have taken the time to read this statement, you likely place value upon fostering a workplace of like-minded individuals, concerned with the social and environmental impact of everyday interactions and decisions. It is assumed that there is considerable interest in the benefits I might provide your organization and it is hoped that this site provides the information necessary to assess integration with your work environment.
